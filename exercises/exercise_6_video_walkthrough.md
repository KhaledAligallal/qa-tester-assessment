# Exercise 6: Video Walkthrough — Live Bug Triage

| Detail     | Value          |
|------------|----------------|
| **Time**   | ~20 minutes    |
| **Points** | 20             |
| **Type**   | Screen recording |

---

## Why This Exercise Exists

This exercise tests your **real QA muscle** — not what you can Google or ask AI. We want to see how you think when you sit down with a new app, what you click first, how you reason about severity, and how you talk through findings.

The single most useful signal we can get from a candidate is watching them test something for the first time.

---

## Task

Record a **5-minute screen recording** (with your voice narrating) where you:

1. **Open** the buggy admin app — either hosted at **https://mazen-salah.github.io/qa-tester-assessment/app/buggy-admin/** or locally via `app/buggy-admin/index.html` (same app used in Exercise 7)
2. **Explore the app** as if you've never seen it before — narrate your first impressions and what you'd test first
3. **Find and demonstrate at least 2 bugs live on camera**
4. **For one of the bugs**, walk through how you'd write it up: severity, repro steps, suspected root cause, and which dev/team to assign it to
5. **Then** open the browser DevTools (Console / Network / Elements tab) and show how you'd dig deeper on a backend-looking issue

---

## Requirements

- **Must include your voice** explaining your thought process
- **Must show DevTools** (Console or Network tab) at some point
- **Must show the actual app** with you clicking around — not just slides
- Keep it under **7 minutes** — we respect your time
- Quality of explanation matters more than number of bugs found

---

## How to Submit

1. Record using **Loom** (free), **OBS**, your OS's built-in screen recorder, or your phone pointed at the screen
2. Upload to one of:
   - YouTube (unlisted)
   - Google Drive (share link with "anyone with link can view")
   - Loom (share link)
3. Put the video link in your `answers/questionnaire_answers.json` file (generated by the questionnaire)
4. Also add the link to your `README.md`

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Points | What We Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Exploration approach | 5 | You have a system — not random clicking. Smoke first, edges next. |
| Bug identification | 5 | Real bugs found live, not invented or recited |
| DevTools usage | 4 | Actually opens Console / Network, not just F12 for show |
| Severity reasoning | 3 | You can justify why a bug is Critical/High/Medium — not just label it |
| Communication | 3 | Calm, clear, would be easy to follow in a stand-up |

---

## Tips

- Don't rehearse a perfect script — we want to see your real thought process
- It's OK to say "I don't know what this is supposed to do, let me try..." — that's authentic and useful
- If you find something unexpected, talk through what you'd ask the PM
- Show your browser, the DevTools panel, and your face/voice — not just the page
- If the app crashes or behaves oddly, **keep recording** — that's exactly what we want to see your reaction to
